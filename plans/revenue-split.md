# Revenue Split: Platform vs Instructor

## Common Revenue Split Models

### 1. Marketplace Revenue Share (Platform brings students)

| Model | Instructor Keeps | Platform Keeps | Used By |
|---|---|---|---|
| Organic sales (platform traffic) | 37–50% | 50–63% | Udemy, eLearning Markets |
| Instructor-driven sales (own promo) | 80–97% | 3–20% | Udemy, Teachng, eLearning Markets |
| Subscription royalty pool | 15–30% (watch-time based) | 70–85% | Skillshare, Udemy Business |
| Flat revenue share (all sales) | 50–80% | 20–50% | Many mid-size platforms |

### 2. SaaS / Self-Hosted Model (Instructor brings students)

| Plan | Monthly Fee | Transaction Fee | Instructor Keeps |
|---|---|---|---|
| Teachable Basic | $39 | 5% | ~92% |
| Teachable Pro | $119 | 0% | ~97% |
| Thinkific Free | $0 | 0% (limited) | ~97% |
| Thinkific Pro | $99 | 0% | ~97% |
| Kajabi Pro | $149 | 0% | ~97% |

### 3. Hybrid Models

- **Tiered splits** — instructors earn higher cuts as they sell more (e.g., 60% → 70% → 80%)
- **Category-based splits** — platform takes less on high-priced categories (e.g., 20% for business, 40% for hobby)
- **Exclusive vs non-exclusive** — lower platform cut if instructor agrees not to publish elsewhere

## Best Practices for Revenue Splitting

1. **Charge based on value provided**
   - If you bring the student (instructor marketing) → keep 85–97%
   - If the platform brings the student → 30–50% platform cut is fair
   - If the platform handles production (video, editing) → adjust accordingly

2. **Be transparent**
   - Show exactly how net revenue is calculated (taxes, payment processing, refunds)
   - Publish clear payout schedules and minimum thresholds

3. **Avoid extreme splits early on**
   - Taking >60% on organic sales drives instructors away before you have critical mass
   - Offering <60% to instructors makes it hard to attract quality content

4. **Consider the full picture**
   - Instructors compare effective take-home, not just headline percentages
   - Include payment processing (~2.9% + $0.30), currency conversion, platform fees in your calculation
   - A "70% split" with hidden fees might be worse than a clear "50% split"

5. **Pay fast**
   - Monthly or bi-weekly payouts build trust
   - 30–90 day holds (Udemy's model) frustrate instructors

## Competitive Suggestions for a New Platform

As a new entrant competing against Udemy (63% cut) and Skillshare (70%+), you have a **window to differentiate**:

### Suggested Split Structure

| Sale Channel | Instructor | Platform | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instructor-driven (coupon/referral) | **95%** | 5% | Best in market — beats Udemy's 97% by being simpler, no tricks |
| Organic (platform traffic) | **60–70%** | 30–40% | Significantly better than Udemy's 37% |
| Subscription / bundle revenue | **40% of pool** | 60% | Double Skillshare's ~20% instructor pool |

### Key Differentiators

- **No surprise discounts** — unlike Udemy, never force courses into flash sales without instructor consent
- **Quick payouts** — pay instructors within 2 weeks instead of 90 days
- **Transparent calculator** — show instructors exactly what they'd earn at every price point
- **Student email access** — let instructors communicate with their students (Udemy blocks this)
- **Instructor-set pricing** — no artificial price caps

### Retention & Growth Tactics

- **Instructor onboarding** — help new instructors produce quality content (free course on course creation)
- **Revenue milestones** — automatically bump instructors to higher split tiers (e.g., 65% → 75% after 100 sales)
- **Exclusivity bonus** — offer an extra 5% if instructors publish exclusively on your platform
- **Referral program** — give instructors 10% of platform fees from other instructors they refer

### To Avoid

| Pitfall | Why |
|---|---|
| Hidden deduction layers | Erodes trust — be upfront about what comes off the top |
| Aggressive discounting without consent | Devalues instructors' work and drives them away |
| Poor instructor support | Word spreads fast among educator communities |
| Locking in student data | Creates adversarial relationship with your content creators |
